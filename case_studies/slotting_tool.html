Improving Fulfilment Workflow Performance

Large-order delivery slotting depended on manual queue checks, creating delays, expectation mismatches, and inconsistent delivery confidence for healthcare operations.

Context

At Zipline, healthcare logistics teams needed reliable ways to coordinate delivery promises across order operations, warehouse work, and contact-centre communication.

Problem

Large-order delivery slotting depended on manual queue checks, creating delays, expectation mismatches, and inconsistent delivery confidence for healthcare operations.

What I did

  • Visited 8 of 13 distribution centres across Ghana, Rwanda, Kenya, and Nigeria to observe the workflow directly.
  • Worked with warehouse operators and contact-centre agents to understand where manual slotting broke down.
  • Mapped the workflow, translated pain points into requirements, and aligned operational stakeholders with engineering trade-offs.
  • Supported staged rollout, training, adoption tracking, and post-launch feedback loops.

Product decisions

  • Solve the repeatable workflow bottleneck rather than add more manual coordination.
  • Prioritise reliability and expectation-setting over a larger first release.
  • Release in stages so lower-volume sites could validate the operating model before broader rollout.

Result

Improved on-time delivery from 77% to 97%, saved 3-4 minutes per large order, and reached 100% adoption among target users within four weeks.

What this proves

  • Eric can turn frontline operational complexity into a practical product workflow.
  • He can combine discovery, prioritisation, rollout, and adoption instead of stopping at requirements.
  • He understands logistics systems from both operator and platform perspectives.

Proof points
