Improving Fulfilment Workflow Performance

Order intake, warehouse priority, delivery capacity, and health-facility SLA expectations were connected by too many manual checks. The product opportunity was to turn that operating reality into a clearer slotting workflow.

Context

At Zipline, healthcare logistics teams needed reliable ways to coordinate delivery promises across order operations, warehouse work, and contact-centre communication. Orders came through contact-centre agents via Freshcaller and WhatsApp, with WhatsApp accounting for about 60% of orders.

Problem

Operators packed based on priority: Emergency, Replenishment, Scheduled, and Custom Window, then by order time. When order volume exceeded scheduling and delivery capacity, teams relied on Excel sheets and flight-controller estimates to understand what could realistically be delivered.

The product problem was not just a queue. It was too many friction points and too much mental math: operators had to check manual trackers, estimate package load, ask flight controllers for best guesses, and judge whether full orders could still meet communicated health-facility SLAs.

What I did

  • Visited 8 of 13 distribution centres across Ghana, Rwanda, Kenya, and Nigeria to observe the workflow directly.
  • Worked with warehouse operators and contact-centre agents to understand where manual slotting broke down.
  • Mapped the workflow, built a prototype, and translated pain points into Jira epics with clear descriptions and acceptance criteria.
  • Worked across discovery, workflow mapping, product description, testing, training, staged rollout, and feedback loops.
  • Helped operators move from manual checks to trusting a suggested packing and delivery-slot sequence.

Product decisions

  • Solve the repeatable workflow bottleneck rather than add more manual coordination.
  • Prioritise reliability and expectation-setting over a larger first release.
  • Automatically arrange orders into delivery and flight slots based on queue position, priority, order time, and capacity signals.
  • Show operators what to pack next and whether full orders could meet communicated health-facility SLAs.
  • Eliminate the need to ask flight controllers for best guesses during normal slotting decisions.
  • Release in stages so lower-volume sites could validate the operating model before broader rollout.

Rollout friction

The main adoption challenge was trust. Operators were used to relying on their own judgment, Excel checks, and flight-controller estimates. Adoption worked because the tool made the work easier and operators had been heavily involved in discovery and testing, so the final workflow was not a surprise.

Result

Improved weekly on-time delivery against health-facility SLAs from 77% to 97%, saved 3-4 minutes per large order by removing Excel checks, flight-controller estimates, and manual SLA calculations, and reached 100% adoption among target users within four weeks.

What this proves

  • I can turn frontline operational complexity into a practical product workflow.
  • I combine discovery, prioritisation, rollout, and adoption instead of stopping at requirements.
  • I understand logistics systems from both operator and platform perspectives.

Proof points
